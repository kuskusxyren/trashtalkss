Mission

Our goal is to help our community by working with neighbors and local leaders to create a better environment for future generations to live and work in. We want to ensure the environmental health and well-being of the community while also safeguarding the social, economic, and ecological health of the area. We primarily focus on remediation of contaminated ecological conditions in canal systems and drainages.

Vision

We have made a long-term commitment to helping improve the environment so that it may support the area's social growth and security, while at the same time ensuring that the environment will last into the future. Furthermore, taking care of the environment will help the community prosper and become more united due to the people working together with a similar goal in mind. Given the current situation we have right now it is necessary to have social interactions and help each other in the midst of the times.

BLOG 1: Trash Talk Time!

Have some trash talk with us! Garbage doesn't go away, so don't believe everything you see. All of our rubbish stays on Earth in one of the following four places: the atmosphere, the ocean, landfills, or recycled. And there is a lot of rubbish that gets made. There are around ten trucks on the road for every one hauling rubbish. That is correct, only one in ten trucks are employed to move rubbish. The worldwide challenge of solid waste management impacts everyone on the planet.

Wastes improperly managed are polluting the world's oceans, causing sewers to back up, and flooding cities. It could spread infections, injure animals that consume trash, and impair the economy in areas such as tourism. This situation is only going to become worse until immediate action is taken.

BLOG 2: Let's Recycle!

What exactly is recycling? Recycling, in other words, reusing things that have already been used is recycling. Many people use the term converting garbage into a new product when referring to recycling. Maintaining the environment is one of the main advantages of recycling.

Recycling materials can lead to environmental sustainability reusing materials that are already available reduces waste, promotes conservation, conserves energy, and minimizes the environmental effect of making new products. To recycle waste is key to determining city, community, and national identity. When it comes to a city's well-being, one with an effective and well-designed recycling system is respected above one that pollutes its surrounds.

“When recycling and sustainability are embraced by the majority, it is critical to stress how it affects sustainability. Sustainability is grounded in recycling since it helps to keep items out of landfills. It's good for the environment since it reduces pollution in ecosystems by, for example, recycling plastics and metals. As a result, the area that was previously contaminated can be reused for other reasons.

Why must you be involved? Everyone must be active since the concept of recycling and the role it plays in environmental improvement necessitates participation from everybody. BLOG 3: The Truth About Irrigation

CAN YOU EXPLAIN WHAT IRRIGATION IS? Irrigation is the process of watering crops by bringing in water through pipelines, canals, sprinklers, or other man-made sources, rather than relying solely on rainfall.

Without irrigation, agriculture would be impossible to sustain in areas with seasonal rainfall patterns. Drought-prone areas benefit from irrigation because it promotes crop growth and quality while reducing crop losses. It also helps to provide more predictable food supplies by allowing farmers to cultivate crops on a steady timetable than would otherwise be possible.

Drought-resistant irrigation canals are the primary streams that transport irrigation water from a water source to the areas that need to be irrigated. They can be lined with concrete, brick, or stone, or they can be lined with a flexible membrane to keep seepage and erosion from occurring.

An open canal, channel, or ditch is a waterway that is open to the public and whose purpose is to transport water from one location to another. Main waterways that deliver water to one or more farms are referred to as channels and canals. Field ditches are smaller in size and are used to transport water from the farm entrance to the irrigated fields on the farm.
